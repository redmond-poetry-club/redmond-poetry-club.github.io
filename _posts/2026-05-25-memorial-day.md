## A New Poem

While we're off, here's something I wrote.

### Metallurgy

> We are born iron,  
> perfect lattice, malleable—  
> also vulnerable to rust.
>
> Life gave us fire,  
> uninvited,  
> with carbon and chromium.
>
> We are burnt. It hurts.  
> The lattice is no longer perfect.
>
> That imperfection, however,  
> is what gives us strength.  
> We can endure stress.
>
> The chromium  
> gives us coating.  
> We can endure moisture.
>
> Life is a bitch. It hurts.  
> But it also strengthens us.
>
> I don't thank the fire,  
> but the fire  
> etched its traces  
> into the lattice  
> until forever.

*This is a poem about steel.*  
*It's also about how being burnt doesn't mean you're ruined—*  
*it means the lattice changed enough to hold more weight than before.*
