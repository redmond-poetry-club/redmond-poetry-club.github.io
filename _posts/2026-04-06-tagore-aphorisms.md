This week we explored the art of saying profound things simply through Tagore's one-line nature poems.

## Rabindranath Tagore — From *Stray Birds* (1916)

Tagore wrote these as single-line poems—aphorisms that hold a nature image and a life truth at once. He was Bengali, Nobel laureate (1913). He self-translated these into English, so what you read is a second original, not a translation.

### Three Poems

> **No. 4:** It is the tears of the earth that keep her smiles in bloom.

*The move:* Suffering and beauty are not opposites. One produces the other.

> **No. 6:** If you shed tears when you miss the sun, you also miss the stars.

*The move:* Grief has a cost you didn't account for. He shows you the sky instead of telling you to stop crying.

> **No. 43:** The fish in the water is silent, the animal on the earth is noisy, the bird in the air is singing. But Man has in him the silence of the sea, the noise of the earth and the music of the air.

*The move:* Builds a taxonomy of three, then collapses it. Humans contain everything.

### How Tagore Works

**Each poem makes exactly one move. That's the discipline. Never two.**

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Paradox flip** | Two opposites. Show they produce each other. |
| **Conditional trap** | If you do X, you also do Y. The cost is hidden until now. |
| **Taxonomy + one** | List the parts. Then show they all live in one thing. |

### Write One Yourself

1. Start with something you believe. A truth, a lesson, a motto.
2. Then forget you believe it.
3. Find it in a tree, a river, a season.
4. Say only that.

**Example:**
> The flowers are beautiful. The trunk was there first.

Foundation before surface. One move.

**The lesson is never stated. The image IS the lesson. If you feel the urge to explain—stop. The poem is done before the explanation.**

## Class Notes & Student Poems

*From our April 6, 2026 class at 3:00 PM*

During class, we tried writing our own Tagore-style aphorisms. Here are some student creations:

### Student Poems

> **Fish do not know water in water,**  
> **and people do not know the air / breath in.**

*The move:* What surrounds us completely becomes invisible. The fish doesn't know it's wet.

> **Our life is same like the Sun—**  
> **rise up and down.**

*Using metaphor to show life's cycles.*

> **A calm heart can cure three thousand ailments,**  
> **and a calm mind can understand the principle of all things.**

*An old Chinese proverb using parallelism—calm heart heals the body, calm mind comprehends truth.*

> **Nature is the best healing.**

> **One flower, one world; one leaf, one Bodhi.**

*A Buddhist saying (一花一世界，一葉一菩提): the tiny flowers and leaves contain infinite cosmic connections. Ordinary things contain the wisdom of enlightenment.*

### From another session:

> **Life is not a run.**  
> **It is a stroll on the trail,**  
> **smelling the fragrance of flowers.**

> **Desire is a well—it is wet and dark.**

> **Time is just time, but when was the charm?**

### Writing Process Notes

The class brainstormed words before writing:
- Desire, Desert, Run, Walk, Goal, Planet, Origin, Aim

> **Caterpillars—it takes a lot of steps to butterfly.**

*A student's Tagore-style observation about transformation.*
